Natural Disasters Analysis

Final Data Analytics Project | Python & Tableau | 1970–2021

Project Overview

This final project analyzes global natural disasters between 1970 and 2021. The project examines how disaster type, country characteristics and economic development level influence the human and economic damage caused by natural disasters.

The goal of the analysis is to support governments and emergency organizations in improving disaster preparedness, resource allocation, infrastructure planning and long-term risk management.

Key Numbers

  • 11,041 natural disaster events between 1970–2021.
  • Approximately 3.3 million fatalities.
  • Approximately 8 billion affected people.
  • Approximately $4.30B in accumulated economic damage.

Main Research Question

What factors influence the severity of natural disasters worldwide, as reflected in the scale of human and economic damage?

Dataset

The project is based on the EM-DAT disaster database, one of the leading and most reliable international sources for studying natural disasters and their effects on populations and economies.

The dataset includes disaster type, country classification, GDP per capita, population size, human impact, economic damage and disaster severity indicators.

Tools Used

Python • Pandas • Data Cleaning • Exploratory Data Analysis • Tableau • Dashboard Design • Data Visualization

Key Insights

  • Floods and storms are the most common natural disasters worldwide.
  • Floods and droughts affect the largest number of people globally.
  • Earthquakes are the deadliest disaster type in terms of fatalities.
  • Storms, earthquakes and floods generate the highest economic damages.
  • Poorer countries show higher human impact on average compared to wealthier countries.
  • Economic damage is affected not only by disaster severity, but also by the country’s level of economic development.

Recommendations

  • Strengthen early warning systems in vulnerable countries.
  • Prioritize resources for disaster types with the highest impact.
  • Use disaster severity indicators to support emergency decision-making.
  • Adapt emergency plans according to each country’s level of economic development.
  • Develop a data-driven risk prediction and disaster management system.
