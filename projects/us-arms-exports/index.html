U.S. Arms Exports Analysis

EDA Project | Python | 2010–2025

Project Overview

This EDA project analyzes U.S. arms exports between 2010 and 2025 using Python. The analysis explores export trends over time, major recipient countries, weapon categories, deal values and delivery patterns.

The project is based on the SIPRI Arms Transfers Database and focuses on understanding how U.S. arms exports reflect security, policy and international trade dynamics.

Dataset

The dataset is based on the SIPRI Arms Transfers Database and includes 4,033 records and 17 columns. It contains information about arms deals, recipient countries, weapon categories, export values and delivery years.

During the preparation stage, the dataset was explored using Python methods such as df.info() and df.describe(), followed by handling missing values and creating a unified deal value field based on TIV and SIPRI estimates.

Research Questions

  • Who are the main recipient countries of U.S. arms exports?
  • How did U.S. arms exports change over time between 2010 and 2025?
  • Which weapon categories are the most common or valuable?
  • Which countries purchase advanced weapon systems compared to basic systems?
  • What is the average delivery time, and how does it vary by country and weapon type?

Tools Used

Python • Pandas • Data Cleaning • Exploratory Data Analysis • Data Visualization

Key Insights

  • U.S. arms exports show an increasing trend over the years.
  • Exports are concentrated among a limited number of key recipient countries with strong strategic ties to the United States.
  • High-value exports are mainly driven by advanced weapon systems such as fighter aircraft, missiles, air defense systems, sensors and detection systems.
  • Basic systems include armored vehicles, artillery, engines, naval systems and operational equipment.
  • Advanced systems usually have longer delivery times due to their complexity.
